Generated: 2026-05-19

## Authority Statement

This packet is a hybrid learning prototype of a modern, paired-course
konghou-inspired harp. It is **not**:

- a culturally authoritative or scholarly konghou design;
- a reconstruction of the Han-era plucked konghou, the vertical konghou, the
  arched konghou, or any other historical konghou variant;
- a representation of any specific Chinese maker, conservatory, school, or
  regional tradition;
- a production-ready or commercial instrument;
- a museum-grade replica or heritage piece;
- a fabrication-ready package — see `public-readiness.md` for the open L3 and
  L4 gates.

It **is**: a modern paired-course revival study, built in the harp-lute
acoustic class, that uses the name "konghou" because that is the family the
design is inspired by, and because honest naming is preferable to a
manufactured euphemism.

## Sources Not Yet Consulted (Open Gaps)

The following classes of source must be consulted before any cultural,
traditional, or heritage claim is made in this repo, in marketing copy, in a
build log, in a public README, or in image captions:

- primary Chinese-language scholarship on konghou history, classification,
  and reconstruction debates;
- museum measurements or published reconstruction notes from named
  institutions (e.g. Shanghai Conservatory, Palace Museum, regional
  collections);
- interviews or correspondence with living konghou makers, modern
  paired-course revival builders, or performers;
- review by at least one Chinese-music practitioner familiar with the modern
  revival instrument;
- review of the chosen English name and any Chinese-character or pinyin
  labelling before it appears on a finished instrument or in marketing.

Until these gaps are closed, this packet is engineering-prototype scope only.

## Cultural-Review Gate

The cultural-review gate is **open**. It must close before any of the
following are permitted in this repo, in any sibling repo, in marketing, in a
public build log, or in image captions:

- the words "traditional", "authentic", "heritage", "historical
  reconstruction", or equivalent phrasings in any language;
- a claim that the instrument represents a named tradition, school, region,
  maker lineage, or period;
- printed or engraved Chinese-character labelling on a finished instrument;
- marketing or commercial framing of any kind;
- public exhibition, performance, or pedagogy framing that names a tradition.

Engineering and shop-internal language ("modern revival study", "paired-course
mule", "konghou-inspired harp") remains permitted while the gate is open.
