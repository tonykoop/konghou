# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A finished modern konghou-inspired concert harp standing upright as a single real physical object, photographed for a product catalog. Its defining feature is the paired-course double-row playing surface: two near-mirrored rows of strings running down the soundboard so each note is doubled, giving the recognizable two-curtain silhouette of a modern konghou. A tall curved hardwood neck rail carries a row of tuning pins along its top edge; a vertical compression column ties the neck back down to the base; the strings descend at a steady angle to a long bridge rail seated on a slender wood soundboard with hitch-pin anchors along the lower frame. Materials read as warm laminated and solid hardwood frame members with a satin hand-rubbed finish, a fine-grained spruce-toned soundboard, and brushed-metal tuning and hitch pins; nylon-and-wound strings catch the light in two parallel planes. Floor-standing concert scale, roughly chest-to-head height, with a stable footed base for scale cues. Clean neutral light-grey studio background, soft directional studio light, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the signature paired-course bridge region where the two parallel rows of strings meet the long bridge rail on the soundboard: show the doubled string planes converging to their anchor line, the bridge cap and bearing line, brushed-metal hitch pins set into the lower frame, and the grain and satin finish of the hardwood frame against the fine-grained soundboard. Raking side light to reveal string spacing, wood grain, and the subtle crown of the soundboard, high detail, materials and textures emphasized, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension lines, callouts, or rulers; no impossible string geometry or floating bridge; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
